TAMU ASTRO GROUP

Research in Astronomy at Texas A&M

The Astronomy group at Texas A&M is an inclusive and supportive community that offers a strong Ph.D. program for observational and theoretical astronomy. Students are able (and expected) to start research projects as soon as they arrive and are encouraged to work with multiple advisors during their education. 1st and 2nd year students are able to dive into research while taking classes many publishing papers and presenting at confrences. This early start gives students a strong foundation in the skills neccesary to succeed in their carreer in academia (or elsewhere).

The research conducted here can be broken into a few general groups: extragalactic, Milky Way, transient astronomy including supernovae and the distance ladder, and instrumentaion.
